Jean Krotzer

February 7, 1915 - September 2, 2005

Jean Young Krotzer, Columbus, Ohio, died after 90 wonderful years, on Friday, September 2, 2005.

She was born February 7, 1915 in Barberton, Ohio to Arthur and Alice Karshner Young.

Jean graduated from Republic High School in 1933 and from Flower Hospital Nursing School in 1936.

She married, Milton Krotzer in 1938. She retired from Tiffin Mental Retardation Center in 1982 where she had been employed as a registered nurse.

Preceding her in death were her husband, Milton, her sisters Elizabeth Hensinger (Neile), Martha Harrison (Dave), and her brother Tom Funk (Pat). Survivors include her son, Bill in Fresno, California; her daughters, Alice Jean Alt (Will) in Plano, Texas, and Ann Myers (Marc) in Columbus, Ohio; grandchildren include Christine Siemens (Johnny), Lisa Arceneaux (Danny), and U. S. Army Sgt. Brian Alt (Patti). Also surviving is great-grandson, Ian Siemens.

Jean was active while in Tiffin at First Lutheran Church in Fidelis Class, Luther Fellowship, and Lydia Circle. She was also actively involved in the Order of Eastern Stars #189, Professional Nurses Club, and the Tiffin Historical Society. In 1997 she moved to Wesley Glen Retirement Center in Columbus, Ohio and joined Gethsemane Lutheran Church.

Visitation will be held on Friday, September 9, 2005 from 11 a.m.-2 p.m. at the ENGLE-SHOOK FUNERAL HOME in Tiffin, Ohio. A Graveside service will follow at 2 p.m., in the Fairmont Cemetery in Tiffin with the Rev. Richard Hofmann officiating.

In lieu of flowers, memorials in Jean's name can be made to the Charitable Care Fund at Wesley Glen Retirement Center, 5155 North High St., Columbus, Ohio 43211, Gethsemane Lutheran Church, 35 E. Stanton Ave., Columbus, Ohio 43214, or First Lutheran Church, 300 Melmore Street, Tiffin, Ohio.
